# Stratos Edge — Documentation Guide

How to write and format Stratos Edge documents — voice, tone, and the two doc profiles to choose between.

> For visual identity (colors, fonts, marks), see [BRAND.md](./BRAND.md).

---

## Voice

Calm, declarative, anti-hype. Stratos Edge talks like a grown-up advisor to boards and C-suites in regulated industries (healthcare, financial services). Not a vendor pitching.

### The five rules

1. **Statements, not claims.** Every sentence should read like something true, not something being argued. "Most AI initiatives fail" works. "We help you avoid failure!" does not.
2. **Strategy before technology.** This is the thesis. Every doc should reinforce that getting AI right is a strategy problem first. "Stop buying AI tools. Start building AI capability."
3. **Short and declarative beats long and qualified.** Three short sentences beat one comma-laden one. The homepage's strongest lines are fragments: "We walk alongside you." / "These aren't technology failures. They're strategy failures."
4. **No hype words.** Avoid: cutting-edge, revolutionary, transformative (used as marketing adjective), best-in-class, world-class, unleash, supercharge, AI-powered (as a brag), seamless, robust, leverage (verb), synergy, paradigm, ecosystem-as-buzzword. If a word would fit on a software sales deck from 2017, cut it.
5. **One coral period per moment.** In writing terms: don't end every paragraph with a punchy declaration. Save the big declarative finish for the moment that actually deserves it. The homepage uses it sparingly — a section title gets one, not every line inside the section.

### Sound of the brand (homepage-derived)

These are the homepage's actual voice samples. Match this register, not "marketing voice."

- "AI advisory for organizations where getting it wrong isn't an option."
- "What we help you do."
- "We walk alongside you."
- "FlightPlan™ is how we do it."
- "However far along you are, you leave more confident in what's next."
- "These aren't technology failures. They're strategy failures — and they're preventable."
- "Stop buying AI tools. Start building AI capability."
- "No pitch deck. No pressure. Just a conversation."

### Vocabulary

- **Use:** advisor, advisory, vendor-neutral, mid-market, regulated industries, board-ready, readiness, strategy, capability, ecosystem, partner, walk alongside, no cost to you.
- **Avoid:** solutions provider (we're an advisor), AI-powered (we don't build AI), cutting-edge, disrupt, game-changer, deep dive, dive in (any dive metaphor), wheelhouse, circle back.
- **Capitalize correctly:** **FlightPlan™** (camelCase, trademark on first major use per doc). **Stratos Edge** (two words, both capitalized). Industries are lowercase in body text ("healthcare," "financial services") unless leading a heading.

### Headline style

- End with a coral period (visually) — see [BRAND.md → The coral period](./BRAND.md#the-coral-period).
- Sentence case, not Title Case. "Who's behind it" not "Who's Behind It."
- Most homepage headlines are 2–6 words. Match that.
- Don't pose questions you immediately answer — make the statement instead. "What we help you do." (statement) vs "What can we help you do?" (weaker).

### Tense and person

- "We" for the company. Never "I" in a document (even from Jeff or Micah — sign at the bottom instead of writing in first person).
- Present tense for what Stratos does. Past for what's happened. Future tense should be rare — Stratos talks about what's true now, not what will be.

## Document profiles

Two profiles cover essentially all Stratos documents. Pick by intent, not by audience.

### Stratos Simple — the default

Most documents are this. Clean, light-backgrounded, Stratos-identified, professionally formatted, easy to read. Not selling anything. Used for confirmations, intro letters, summary docs, internal memos, working notes, explainers, short briefs.

**Visual recipe:**

- Background: `#FFFFFF` (default) or `#F2F0E9` (warm editorial)
- Header: small `STRATOS | EDGE` wordmark (logo black PNG, sized to ~32px tall), left-aligned, top of first page
- Body: Lora, 1.0625rem–1.125rem, line-height 1.55–1.7, color INK `#04242D`
- Section headings: Work Sans 400, INK, with the coral period
- Eyebrow labels (optional): Work Sans 500, 0.875rem, UPPERCASE, letter-spacing 0.12em, coral
- One CTA or contact line at the end, never throughout
- Footer: a single line of small Lora at MUTED — date, page number if multi-page, and the contextual identifier (see Wording variants below)

**What this profile is NOT:**
- No hero cover page. Open straight to the document.
- No accent bar, no stat strips, no numbered insight blocks. Those are Playbook moves.
- No dark teal sections.
- No coral-gradient buttons. If there's a CTA, it's a single coral-period line of text (e.g., "Reply to confirm" or "Book a call at stratosedge.ai/contact.").

**Wording variants (within Stratos Simple):**

| Use | Footer line | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Internal-facing (Stratos team, advisors) | `Stratos Edge · Internal · {date}` | No need for the word "Confidential" unless the document genuinely contains material you'd be embarrassed to leak. Date is enough to signal currency. |
| Client/recipient-facing | `Stratos Edge · {date}` or `Stratos Edge · stratosedge.ai` | Date or website. No "Confidential" stamp. |
| Public-facing | `© 2026 Stratos Edge LLC · stratosedge.ai` | Use only on docs you'd hand to anyone. |

The styling is identical across all three. The footer copy is the only variable.

### Stratos Playbook — the intentional moment

For documents that are doing real persuasive work: sales playbooks, lead magnets, the agent program opportunity doc, methodology overviews, industry briefs. Borrows the FlightPlan deliverable design language — colorized, sectioned, editorial, dramatic.

Use this profile sparingly. It's the equivalent of a Stratos hero band — high impact when used, exhausting if everything looks like this.

**Visual recipe:**

- **Cover page**: full-bleed dark teal `#04242D` background. Small coral eyebrow ("AGENT PROGRAM," "SALES PLAYBOOK") in Work Sans uppercase at the top. Large Lora 400 hero title, white, with letter-spacing `-0.02em`. Subtitle in Lora regular at `rgba(245, 245, 247, 0.65)`. Wordmark white at the bottom corner.
- **Section dividers**: full-bleed dark teal bands between sections, mirroring FlightPlan's pattern. Coral eyebrow + large Lora title in white. Use 2–4 of these in a doc, not on every section.
- **Body sections**: alternate `#FFFFFF` and `#F8F8F8` (and occasionally `#F2F0E9` for editorial moments). Work Sans 400 headings with coral period. Lora body.
- **Components available**: accent bar under cover title, eyebrow labels above sections, stat strips, pull quotes, numbered insight blocks. Use 1–2 component types per doc — not all of them.
- **CTA**: end with a single coral-gradient button (`linear-gradient(135deg, #FF6B4A 0%, #FF8A6B 100%)`) and a one-line action statement.
- **Footer on body pages**: small Work Sans line — `Stratos Edge · stratosedge.ai · page N`. On internal-only playbooks (sales playbook for the team), substitute `Stratos Edge · Internal · page N`. Don't use "Confidential" unless the document genuinely warrants it.

**When to choose Playbook over Simple:**
- The reader is supposed to feel something (interest, urgency, conviction) by the end.
- The doc is going to be printed, screenshotted, or shared as a marketing artifact.
- The doc is making an argument, not delivering information.

Everything else is Simple.

## Components

Reusable visual elements that appear inside documents. All are optional — pick what serves the content.

### Section heading

```
[ EYEBROW LABEL ]                 ← optional, coral, uppercase, tracked
Section title.                     ← Work Sans 400, coral period, sentence case
[Body paragraph follows in Lora.]
```

### Pull quote

A short, bold statement that interrupts body flow.

- 4px CORAL left border
- Background CREAM `#F2F0E9` or OFFWHITE `#F8F8F8`
- Padding `1.5rem 2rem`
- Margin `2rem 0`
- Lora 400, italic, size `1.25rem`–`1.5rem`, color INK

**Use sparingly.** One pull quote per major section, max two per Playbook doc.

### Emphasis line

A two-line statement where the second line carries the punch.

```
The real problem isn't AI.
It's the plan.        ← second line, coral, weight 500
```

Work Sans 300 throughout, with the punch phrase in coral at weight 500. Used in Playbook docs for argument framing.

### Stat strip

A horizontal row of 2–4 large numbers with small labels beneath. Used in Playbook docs to anchor an argument with data.

```
95%                70%                97%
of AI pilots       of failures        of workforce
fail               are governance     uses AI poorly
```

- Numbers: Work Sans 200, ~`clamp(3rem, 6vw, 4rem)`, CORAL
- Labels: Work Sans 500, 0.75rem, UPPERCASE, letter-spacing 0.08em, MUTED
- One per major section, never two in the same view

### Numbered insight block

A numbered argument or principle, with the number as a large, low-opacity background mark.

```
01    Section title goes here.
      Body text follows. Lora, regular weight, MUTED for body.
```

- Number: Work Sans 700, `clamp(3.5rem, 5vw, 5rem)`, CORAL at 25–30% opacity, behind/beside the title
- Title: Work Sans 600, `1.25rem`, INK
- Body: Lora, `1.0625rem`, color MUTED

Playbook-only. Used for ordered lists of substantive points (e.g., "Three things every AI strategy needs").

### Accent bar

Short coral gradient bar, used as a divider beneath a cover title or between sections.

```
Width: 44px · Height: 3px · Border-radius: 2px
Background: linear-gradient(135deg, #FF6B4A 0%, #FF8A6B 100%)
```

Playbook-only on covers. Optional between major sections.

### Status row

For documents that need a state badge in the header — drafts, previews, confidentials.

```
● DRAFT — internal review              [right side: date / version]
```

- 6×6px filled circle, color depends on state (`#0F9D6E` for live/confidential, `#E6A61C` for draft/preview)
- Work Sans 500, 0.65rem, UPPERCASE, letter-spacing 0.18em, color MUTED

## Layout

### Page geometry

- US Letter (8.5 × 11 in) for PDFs
- Horizontal margins: 0.7in (Playbook) or 0.85in (Simple — more generous, reads more like an editorial document)
- First-page top padding: 0.55in (Playbook cover) or 0.85in (Simple)
- Footer: absolute-positioned at bottom, 0.5in above page edge, with a 1px HAIRLINE rule above on Simple docs, or none on Playbook dark sections

### On screen (HTML rendering of a doc)

- Use `max-width: 760px` for body-text sections (matches About / FAQ rewrite pattern)
- Use `max-width: 1280px` for hero sections and content with photos/grids (matches Homepage pattern)
- Horizontal padding: `px-6 md:px-12 lg:px-16 xl:px-24` (Tailwind, matches site)
- Section padding (vertical): `clamp(5rem, 10vh, 7rem)` for Simple, `clamp(6rem, 12vh, 8rem)` for Playbook hero bands

## Choosing a profile (quick guide)

| Document | Profile |
|---|---|
| Email confirmation page | Simple |
| Intro letter to a prospect | Simple |
| Summary of a discovery conversation | Simple |
| Working memo to the team | Simple (internal variant) |
| Short explainer for a recipient | Simple |
| Brand guide / docs (like this file) | Simple |
| Agent program overview PDF | Playbook |
| Sales Playbook | Playbook |
| Lead magnet / 4 Decisions PDF | Playbook |
| LinkedIn Playbook | Playbook |
| FlightPlan deliverable | (its own thing — uses Playbook patterns at maximum intensity, with full FlightPlan design system) |

When in doubt: **Simple**. It's almost always the right call. Playbook is the exception.

## What this guide is not

- Not a visual identity reference. For palette / fonts / marks, see [BRAND.md](./BRAND.md).
- Not a tone of voice for *spoken* communication (Jeff's emails, Micah's calls). Those are warmer and more personal than written brand voice should be. This guide is for documents.
- Not legal / regulatory copy guidance. Terms of service, privacy policy, and the Agent Program Terms use legal voice (precise, comprehensive, slightly stiff). That's correct for those documents — they're not "Stratos brand voice" exceptions, they're a different genre.
