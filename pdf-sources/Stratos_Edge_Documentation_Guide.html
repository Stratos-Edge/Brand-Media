Documentation Guide.

Voice, doc profiles, and how to write & format Stratos Edge materials.

Voice.

Calm, declarative, anti-hype. Stratos Edge talks like a grown-up advisor to boards and C-suites in regulated industries. Not a vendor pitching.

The five rules

1Statements, not claims.
Every sentence should read like something true, not something being argued. "Most AI initiatives fail" works. "We help you avoid failure!" does not.
2Strategy before technology.
This is the thesis. Every doc should reinforce that getting AI right is a strategy problem first. "Stop buying AI tools. Start building AI capability."
3Short and declarative beats long and qualified.
Three short sentences beat one comma-laden one. The homepage's strongest lines are fragments: "We walk alongside you." / "These aren't technology failures. They're strategy failures."
4No hype words.
Avoid: cutting-edge, revolutionary, transformative (as marketing adjective), best-in-class, world-class, unleash, supercharge, AI-powered (as a brag), seamless, robust, leverage (verb), synergy, paradigm. If a word would fit on a 2017 SaaS sales deck, cut it.
5One coral period per moment.
In writing terms: don't end every paragraph with a punchy declaration. Save the big declarative finish for the moment that actually deserves it.

Vocabulary

Use

advisor, advisory, vendor-neutral, mid-market, regulated industries, board-ready, readiness, strategy, capability, ecosystem, partner, walk alongside, no cost to you.

Avoid

solutions provider (we're an advisor), AI-powered (we don't build AI), cutting-edge, disrupt, game-changer, deep dive, dive in (any dive metaphor), wheelhouse, circle back.

Capitalization

  • FlightPlan™ — camelCase, trademark on first major use per doc.
  • Stratos Edge — two words, both capitalized.
  • Industries are lowercase in body text ("healthcare," "financial services") unless leading a heading.

Headline style

  • End with a coral period (visually) — see the Brand Guide.
  • Sentence case, not Title Case. "Who's behind it" not "Who's Behind It."
  • Most homepage headlines are 2–6 words. Match that.
  • Don't pose questions you immediately answer — make the statement instead.

Tense and person

  • "We" for the company. Never "I" in a document.
  • Present tense for what Stratos does. Past for what's happened. Future tense should be rare.

Document profiles.

Two profiles cover essentially all Stratos documents. Pick by intent, not by audience.

The default Stratos Simple.
Clean, light, Stratos-identified. Used for almost everything.

Most documents are this. Light-backgrounded, Stratos-identified, professionally formatted, easy to read. Not selling anything. Used for confirmations, intro letters, summary docs, internal memos, working notes, explainers, short briefs.

Visual recipe

  • Background: #FFFFFF (default) or #F2F0E9 (warm editorial)
  • Small STRATOS | EDGE wordmark, top of first page, left-aligned
  • Body: Lora 16–18pt, line-height 1.55–1.7, INK #04242D
  • Section headings: Work Sans 400, INK, with the coral period
  • Footer: single line of small Lora at MUTED — date, page number, contextual identifier

What this profile is NOT

  • No hero cover page. Open straight to the document.
  • No accent bar, no stat strips, no numbered insight blocks. Those are Playbook moves.
  • No dark teal sections.
  • No coral-gradient buttons. CTAs are a single line of coral-period text.

The intentional moment Stratos Playbook.
Full marketing treatment. Borrows the FlightPlan deliverable language.

For documents doing real persuasive work: sales playbooks, lead magnets, the agent program opportunity doc, methodology overviews, industry briefs. Borrows the FlightPlan deliverable design language — colorized, sectioned, editorial, dramatic.

Use sparingly
It's the equivalent of a Stratos hero band — high impact when used, exhausting if everything looks like this.

Visual recipe

  • Cover page — full-bleed dark teal #04242D. Small coral eyebrow at top. Large Lora 400 hero title, white, letter-spacing -0.02em. Subtitle in Lora at rgba(245,245,247,0.65). Wordmark white at bottom corner.
  • Section dividers — full-bleed dark teal bands between sections, mirroring FlightPlan. Coral eyebrow + large Lora title in white. 2–4 per doc, not on every section.
  • Body sections — alternate #FFFFFF and #F8F8F8 (occasionally #F2F0E9 for editorial). Work Sans 400 headings with coral period. Lora body.
  • Components available — accent bar under cover title, eyebrow labels above sections, stat strips, pull quotes, numbered insight blocks. Use 1–2 component types per doc — not all of them.
  • CTA — end with a single coral-gradient button and a one-line action statement.
  • Footer on body pages — small Work Sans line, Stratos Edge · stratosedge.ai · page N. Substitute "Internal" for internal-only playbooks. Don't use "Confidential" unless the document genuinely warrants it.

When to choose Playbook over Simple

  • The reader is supposed to feel something (interest, urgency, conviction) by the end.
  • The doc is going to be printed, screenshotted, or shared as a marketing artifact.
  • The doc is making an argument, not delivering information.

Everything else is Simple.

Components.

Reusable visual elements that appear inside documents. All optional — pick what serves the content.

Pull quote

A short, bold statement that interrupts body flow. One per major section, max two per Playbook doc.

They just started. And they started with a plan.

4pt CORAL left border · CREAM or OFFWHITE bg · Lora 400 italic, ~15pt, INK

Emphasis line

A two-line statement where the second line carries the punch.

The real problem isn't AI.
It's the plan.

Work Sans 300 throughout, punch phrase in coral at weight 500

Layout.

Page geometry

  • US Letter (8.5 × 11 in) for PDFs
  • Horizontal margins: 0.7in (Playbook) or 0.85in (Simple — more generous, reads more editorial)
  • First-page top padding: 0.55in (Playbook cover) or 0.85in (Simple)
  • Footer: absolute-positioned, 0.5in above edge; 1pt HAIRLINE rule above on Simple, none on Playbook dark sections

On screen (HTML rendering of a doc)

  • max-width: 760px for body-text sections (matches About / FAQ pattern)
  • max-width: 1280px for hero sections, photos, grids (matches Homepage)
  • Horizontal padding: px-6 md:px-12 lg:px-16 xl:px-24
  • Section padding: clamp(5rem, 10vh, 7rem) Simple · clamp(6rem, 12vh, 8rem) Playbook hero

What this guide is not.

  • Not a visual identity reference. For palette, fonts, marks — see the Brand Guide.
  • Not a tone for spoken communication. Jeff's emails and Micah's calls are warmer and more personal than written brand voice should be. This guide is for documents.
  • Not legal / regulatory copy guidance. Terms of service, privacy policy, the Agent Program Terms use legal voice (precise, comprehensive, slightly stiff). That's correct for those documents — a different genre, not a Stratos brand exception.
